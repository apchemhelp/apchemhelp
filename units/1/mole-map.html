Unit 1: Chemical Analysis

~Mole Map

Before getting to the mole map, let's first focus on the mole. The mole is a way to describe how many molecules of a certain element or compound exist, just like a dozen means 12. In terms of moles, one mole is equivalent to 6.02 x 1023 molecules. The mole is a common way for scientists to address how many molecules of an element or compound there are.

Now that we have a basic understanding of what a mole is, let's talk about the mole map. The mole is like the central control unit, everything has to go through the mole in order to transfer into everything else. My Chem teacher described the mole like the City of Sacramento, if you want to go to San Franscico, Los Angeles, or Reno, you have to go through Sacramento. But quite frankly, most people don't actually care about Sacramento itself, but where can it leads us to.
