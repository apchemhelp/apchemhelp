Scientific Notation

Let’s say you need to write 1, would you bother to write in what we call “scientific notation” form? Which is 1*100. Probably not. But what if you need to express 21 trillion (21,000,000,000,000), which is less than the U.S. National Debt as of 8:15 PM on Friday February 4th, 2022. But anyway, you will probably prefer to write in the scientific notation 2.4*1013

So let’s take a look at the format of scientific notation. It’s basically a number (greater than or equal to one and less than 10) multiplied by 10 to its power. So for example, 2.4*1013 means move the decimal point of 2.4 to the right side for 13 digits. 

You generally used the scientific notation when a number is extremely big or extremely small.
