What’s Chemistry?

Before we talk about anything related to AP Chemistry, let’s talk about chemistry itself. An obvious question, then, will be what is chemistry? Chemistry is the study of properties, behaviors, and interactions between different matters. Have you ever wondered why something dissolves in water? How do plants use photosynthesis to gain energy, or why do rainbows have different colors? These can all be explained using chemistry. 

Let’s take a walkthrough of what you will learn after taking AP Chemistry.

Unit 1: Chemical Analysis:

  • How scientists communicate with each other to ensure data is accurate.
  • To write a chemical equation and balance it
  • The inner workings of spectroscopy.

Unit 2: Gases:

  • How gases behave under the Ideal Gas Law and their respective pressures and volumes.
  • How some gas molecules effuse faster than others.

Unit 3: Atomic Theory:

  • How atoms behave and interact with each other. 
  • How energy is transmitted.
  • How electrons are transferred and shared between atoms.

Unit 4: Periodicity:

  • Use the periodic table to predict the trend and tendency of different properties (such as atomic radii, metallic, etc.) in different atoms.

Unit 5: Bonding:

  • Different shapes and geometry affect a molecule’s likability to interact with other molecules, and how to create said shapes. 
  • Different shapes and geometry influence the probability of a molecule interacting with other molecules.
  • Types of interactions.

Unit 6: Intermolecular force:

  • What are the forces controlling different molecules 
  • The force that affects their properties (such as solubility). 

Unit 7: Kinetics:

  • How fast or slow a chemical reaction happens
  • How to predict a mechanism for a chemical reaction

Unit 8: Equilibrium:

  • How a chemical reaction can achieve equilibrium
  • How equilibrium will affect the chemical reaction.
